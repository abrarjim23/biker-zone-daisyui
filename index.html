Frequently Asked Questions

What are the most important things I should know about riding a bike?

Safety First! Always obey the rules of the road. Obey all traffic signals, signs, and laws. Get in the mindset of “driving” your bike—not just “riding” your bike. This will help you be a more focused and legally compliant bike rider.

How can I tell if my helmet is old and I need a new one?

If your helmet is over **3-5 years old**, has **cracks, dents, or a damaged foam liner**, or has been in an accident, it’s time to replace it. Worn-out straps, deteriorating padding, and prolonged exposure to **sunlight or chemicals** can also weaken its protective abilities. If you notice any of these signs, getting a new helmet is the safest option. 🚴‍♂️🏍️

My bike has been in storage is it safe to ride?

Check the **tires, brakes, chain, and frame** for damage or rust. Inflate the tires, lubricate the chain, and test the brakes before riding. If anything seems off, get a tune-up. 🚴‍♂️

What rules should I follow when riding my bike?

Wear a **helmet**, follow **traffic laws**, use **hand signals**, stay **visible**, and ride **predictably**. Check your **brakes and tires** before riding, avoid distractions, and stay aware of your surroundings. 🚴‍♂️
